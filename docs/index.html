It is important to remember a number, a score, or even a collection or bean hill of scores or numbers, do not tell the whole story of your community engaged research. This framework is provided to help you tell that story using a system that is easily recognizable and considered a valid form of evidence.

It provides an overall reflection on your work and where you have focused your energies. CER-BEANS gives you an indication of the impact your work has had, allowing you to discuss your successes.

It provides a snapshot of where you are in a longer process, what has worked well so far, and where you will be focusing your efforts in the near future.

It provides a rough map of where your and your collaborators, partners, and participants can take the work moving forward.

CER-BEANS focuses on five areas, or shall we say, hills, and they each result in an area score. Each of the area scores contribute to the overall CER-BEANS score. These are the five areas:

  1. Participants
  2. Engagement
  3. Infrastructures
  4. Outputs
  5. Sustainability

Each of the area scores (\(S_a\)) follow a predictable pattern, that includes some impact number (\(I_a\)) that is amplified by area component ratings (\(r_a\)). Here is the general formula for an area score:

\[ S_a = I_a \times (1 + \frac{\sum r_{a1} \ldots r_{an}}{|R_a|}) \]

contribute to the individual area scores, each of which contribute to the overall CER-BEANS score.

Calculate Engagement Score

\(e_s = e_h \times \frac{\sum{e_d, e_f}}{2}\) 
engagement_average <- round(((duration_score + frequency_score) / 2), 2)
